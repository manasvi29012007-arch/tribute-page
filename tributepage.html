Tribute to Virat Kohli

About Virat Kohli

Virat Kohli was born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India. Raised in a middle-class Punjabi family, his early life revolved around education, values, and a love for sports passed down from his father, Prem Kohli. From a very young age, Virat showed an exceptional interest in cricket. Encouraged by his family, he joined the West Delhi Cricket Academy at the age of nine, where his natural talent and fierce determination quickly set him apart from his peers. Virat Kohli continues to influence others through: Inspiring millions with his dedication, confidence, and fitness revolution in Indian cricket Setting global standards for consistency, chasing under pressure, and modern leadership Being included in educational material as a prime example of sportsmanship, resilience, and youth icons.

Achievements

  • Became captain of the Indian Under-19 cricket team, leading them to victory in the 2008 ICC Under-19 World Cup
  • Set records as one of the fastest batsmen to reach 8,000, 10,000, and 12,000 runs in One Day Internationals (ODIs)
  • Captained India to their first-ever Test series win in Australia in 2018-19, and became India’s most successful Test captain with 40 wins in 68 matches
  • Named ICC ODI Cricketer of the Year three times (2012, 2017, 2018) and Test Player of the Year (2018)
  • Top Indian honors including the Khel Ratna,Padma Shri, Arjuna Award and Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna Award.
  • Broke Sachin Tendulkar’s record with 50 ODI centuries, a world record as of 2023
  • Became the highest run-scorer in Indian Premier League (IPL) history and led Royal Challengers Bangalore to their first IPL title in 2025
